// CHALLENGES YOU FACE

Why UAE Businesses Struggle with Cloud Adoption

Moving to the cloud without the right partner exposes your business to hidden costs, compliance risks, and operational disruptions that can offset every benefit you hoped to gain.

01
Complex Migration & Downtime Risk

Poorly planned cloud migrations result in data loss, extended downtime, and broken integrations that disrupt daily operations. Without a structured migration methodology and tested rollback procedures, moving your workloads to the cloud becomes a high-stakes gamble for your business continuity.

02
Uncontrolled Cloud Costs

Without proper cloud governance, over-provisioned resources, forgotten workloads, and inefficient licensing inflate monthly bills far beyond initial projections. UAE businesses consistently overspend on cloud by 30–40% due to misconfigured resources and the absence of cost optimisation policies and regular usage reviews.

03
Compliance & Data Residency

UAE organisations subject to NESA, CBUAE, DIFC, and sector-specific regulations must ensure that sensitive data remains within approved jurisdictions and that cloud configurations meet prescribed security standards. Non-compliant cloud deployments expose organisations to regulatory penalties and reputational damage.

04
Lack of In-House Cloud Expertise

Managing Office 365, Google Workspace, private cloud, and VPS environments requires specialised skills most UAE SMEs cannot afford to maintain in-house. Gaps in cloud administration knowledge lead to misconfigurations, security vulnerabilities, and missed optimisation opportunities that accumulate over time.

05
Inadequate Backup & Recovery

Many businesses mistakenly assume their cloud provider handles backups — in reality, Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace offer limited native recovery options that do not meet enterprise RPO and RTO requirements. Without dedicated cloud backup solutions, accidental deletion, ransomware, and data corruption events lead to permanent data loss.

06
Integration & Vendor Lock-In

Connecting cloud platforms to on-premises systems, legacy applications, and third-party tools requires careful architecture planning. Poor integration design creates data silos, manual workarounds, and over-dependence on a single vendor — limiting your flexibility to adapt as your business requirements evolve.

// HOW WE WORK

Our Cloud Migration & Onboarding Process

A structured five-phase approach from discovery to live managed cloud — delivering a seamless transition with zero data loss and zero disruption to your business operations.

01
Discovery & Cloud Assessment

We audit your existing infrastructure, applications, data volumes, and regulatory requirements — mapping every workload to the most suitable cloud platform, identifying dependencies, and producing a detailed migration plan with realistic timelines and risk assessments before a single system is touched.

02
Architecture & Design

We design your cloud architecture — specifying tenancy configurations, identity and access policies, network connectivity, security controls, backup policies, and governance frameworks — producing a complete technical design reviewed and approved by your team before implementation begins.

03
Migration & Deployment

Our certified engineers execute the migration during planned maintenance windows — migrating mailboxes, files, virtual machines, and data to the target cloud platform using proven tooling with real-time progress monitoring, tested rollback procedures, and direct communication with your team throughout the process.

04
Testing & Validation

Every migrated workload is thoroughly tested — validating data integrity, application functionality, user access, backup recovery, and performance against agreed benchmarks. We conduct user acceptance testing with your team and confirm all systems meet requirements before signing off the migration as complete.

05
Managed Support & Optimisation

Post-migration, we provide ongoing managed cloud support — administering your Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace tenancy, monitoring server and cloud performance, managing licences and costs, applying updates, and delivering monthly reports so your leadership always has full visibility of your cloud environment and expenditure.
